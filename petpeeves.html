Pet Peeves

In other words, my project ideas

Pet Peeve 1

I work at a hotel, and sometimes I'm away from the desk, I could even be on a separate floor. It would be nice if I had a remote way to know if a guest was at the desk.

So I'm imagining a device that gets pinged whenver someone is at the desk. I could use an infrared scanner or a laser, then send a wireless signal to a pager or possibly to my phone.

Pet Peeve 2

I'd like to monitor my own brain activity. You can buy ready-to-go EEGs off of Ebay or Amazon now, but it would be fun to build my own. I'd like to record my delta, theta, alpha, beta, and gamma waves when I'm sleeping, when I'm on/off brain-altering medications, and possibly throughout the day, like doing homework or reading or sitting in lecture (if my professors would allow me to sit there with wires sticking out of my head...)

But then what could I do for output? Sound is the most exciting. But it could also be mapped to visuals, like color, so that different frequencies are mapped to different RGB values, so as the brain transitions from frequencies during sleep, you'll see a gradient of color when you wake up. There are other potential outputs. I like sound best.

Pet Peeve 3

I'd like to learn to play a theremin. But the only one here is the one that it's in the sound lab upstairs of the ATLS building, and I'd like to have one for myself.

I could build my own theremin. But this project has been done before and outputting sound isn't too different from the more exciting EEG brain-music project idea.

Pet Peeve 4

I don't want to pay for a guitar tuner. Right now, I'm using my phone, but I hate the apps, and sometimes the bass tuner doesn't work so well because of the low frequencies, I guess.

I could make my own guitar and bass tuner. But this has been done before and is still less exciting than the EEG brain-music idea.
